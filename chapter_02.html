Exercise 2.5

  1. \(P(\text{selected tree has mold}) = 18\%\)

prob_mold_no_mold <- c(0.18, 0.92)
prob_maple_mold_no_mold <- c(0.8, 0.1)
sum(prob_mold_no_mold * prob_maple_mold_no_mold)
[1] 0.236

\(P(\text{selecting a maple tree}) = 23.6\%\)

prior <- c(0.18, 0.92)
likelihood <- c(0.8, 0.1)
posterior <- prior * likelihood / sum(prior * likelihood)
posterior
[1] 0.6101695 0.3898305

\(P(\text{has mold | maple tree}) = 61.0\%\)

  1. The prior probability of having mold is \(18\%\). However, with the knowledge that the selected tree is a maple, the posterior probability of having mold increased to \(61\%\).
